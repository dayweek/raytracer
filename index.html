Bump Mapping

Position: floor in the foreground and wall on the left
Bump mapping is a technique for making surface appear rought. This is done with height map (bump texture) or normal map. I used bump map. Evything is implemented as a shader that is child of Texture Shader witd additional texture for performing perturbation. The perturbation is done by rotating face normal according to the bump texture - therefore we override getNormal method.
First we set pu and pv vectors in object space. They lie in a plane defined by surface and cross product of these two vectors results in normal. To compute these vectors we linearly transform pu (1,0,0) and pv (0,1,0) form texture space (details in source code). Strongth of perturbation in x is defined as derivative of height map in x axis. We simply take difference between 2 heights in texture space.

perturbated_pu = pu + normal * derivative_in_x
perturbated_normal = pu % pv

pu and pv don't have to be perpendicular after transformation. but in this implementation we assume they are

Reflective and Refractive Transparency

Position: Sphere on the right
Refraction is the change in direction of a wave caused by a change of speed. Light travels in different environments with different speed. Refraction is described by Snell's law that shows relation between direction of refracted ray and incoming ray. Snell's law is implemented as well as ray intensities - not all the light is being refracted but some part is reflected. All code is in RRPhongShader (RR stands for refraction and reflection). Since we shoot every time 2 rays again (except for total internal reflection), we could accidently end up in infinite cycle. Thus before continuing shooting we store intensity of the shooted ray in Integrator. If the intensities in the integrator drop below certain value, integrator will not compute indirect radiance.
